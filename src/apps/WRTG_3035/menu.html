The basecamp menu speaks of simplistic design implemented well. The menu reacts to player inputs and offers discoverability in the highlighting of the four menu options below the title. The buttons are brought to the front and change from grey to white. They include subtext fields that offer quick identifiers to the usefulness of using this object. Here we can see that "upgrades are available" and we might want to upgrade a weapon.
The action stages here are very clear.
I want to travel to another place, Fast travel. It also includes as signifier of your game progress. 30 destinations mean 30 basecamps found.
I want to improve Lara, Skills. Right now I need more experience to gain more skill.

The Main menu
What do I want to accomplish? Options are clearly listed and when activated offer a context that gives immediate feedback to the action that can be performed and the status of selecting this action.
Consistent mapping in this game teaches us that grey text is a signifier of an action we can perform, and the white text is the currently selected action.
The constraint of the menu being the only interactive object forces us to interact with it.
